"Allow Working From Home Will Decrease Employee's Burn Out Rate"

Background:

Our company recently did a survey about Career, work environment and well-being on questions such as:

Im able to manage my work in a way that allows me to maintain a healthy balance between work and home I believe that my health and well-being is a priority As a long time employee, I have a feel about the numbers collected and suspected that one of the factors that has causal connection is WFH setup. Using "https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/blurredmachine/are-your-employees-burning-out" as a model dataset, specifically the train.csv table, I have conducted few tests/plots to test out below hypothesis.

Hypothesis and analysis:

My hypothesis is that allow WFH will have lower Mental Fatigue Score and Burn Rate, which will translate into higher number on work/home balance and health/well-being as a priority suvey results.

I will be analyzing what factors, including Gender, WFH Setup Available, Designation, Resource Allocation, have causal connections to Burn Rate/Mental Fatigue and test out if hypothesis of WFH lower Burn Rate/Mental Fatigue is null or not.

After analysis various working conditions on the current pandemic situation and employee's Mental Fatigue Score and Burn Rate, we can see that the Tenure actually has the biggest impact but that is not something we can change since tenure also brings in experiences/pocket knowledge for the company.
